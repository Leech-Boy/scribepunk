--- layout: post title: "Water is wet and I'm tired of pretending it's not" subtitle: "Fight me" categories: substack ---

To those who are not “in-the-know” this title may be confusing. Perhaps this is a controversy unique to my generation. The issue at stake is that there are those who believe that water is not wet. I have no objection to this belief, but the argument that leads people to accept it is frustrating to me, as is the attitude adopted by those who present it.

The argument goes like this. A person who is in-the-know approaches their victim and asks the seemingly innocuous philosophical question, “Is water wet?”. Their victim thinks and then responds, usually to say, “of course, it’s water”. After this their faithless friend reveals that they have played them for a fool and had no intention of reasonable debate. This traitor then says something to the effect of “Well… according to [The OED/The Merriam Webster Dictionary/Dictionary.com/etc] the definition of ‘wet’ is being [covered by/saturated with] [water/liquid/etc]”. What is introduced as a philosophical debate is actually revealed to be a semantic squabble, or rather something worse, a semantic ambush. Those who fall victim to this assault often soothe their pride by playing this trick on another poor soul. Not me though. The anti-intellectuals who play this trick have made one crucial error. They assume that I care. I have no intention of letting a dictionary tell me what wet is. I know all about wet. I feel it all the time. If Merriam Webster himself came to tell me that I was wrong, I wouldn’t listen. I know what wet is. This reliance on a dictionary definition to carry the entirety of your philosophical argument displays a fundamental misunderstanding of the nature of dictionaries. Dictionaries are descriptive, not prescriptive. That is, a dictionary does not hold the objective, ultimate, and true meanings of every word in a particular language, it describes what the dictionary’s editors believe are the commonly accepted uses of words. Any dictionary that claims to be prescriptive, that is one that gives only the “proper” and “true” definitions of words is merely a descriptive dictionary that only accounts for the accepted uses of words among the intellectual elite, who have no more write to determine the meanings of words than anyone else. If anything, they have less right to declare the meanings of words because they are the minority, and language belongs to the people. Now, if I reject the dictionary definition of “wet”, I am obliged to provide a reasonable definition. When I say “wet” I am referring to the ability of an object to confer the experience of “wetness”. Water is wet because when I touch it, I feel wetness. A sufficiently small amount of water cannot be said to be wet. For example, a single molecule of water is not wet because I will not sense it if I touch it. For that matter, a hundred molecules of water likely cannot be said to be wet. There is some dividing line at which adding another water molecule to a cluster of water makes that water wet. I do not know what that limit is, and it is irrelevant. The statement “water is not wet”, is one that conveys no information. Anyone who agrees with the speaker on the definition of wetness will understand that the statement is a tautology. Anyone who disagrees on the definition of “wet” will be confused. However, the statement “water is wet”, is one that conveys more information. It is not a simple tautology. It is an allusion to that fact that most collections of water molecules that we are aware of on a regular basis have the ability to create a sensation of wetness when touched. For this reason, I believe that my definition of “wet” allows for more a more interesting discussion of this topic. However, I mean not here to convince you, the reader, to agree with my definition of wetness. I really don’t care what you think about it. The important thing here is not the wetness of water, nor is it the correctness of any definition of “wet”. The important thing is to recognize that no dictionary is a prescriptive authority. Rejecting this claim to authority when you encounter it is important. It is irresponsible to submit to illegitimate authorities without deep consideration. Non-conformity is a muscle, and like any other it must be exercised for your own health. This is a practice that many people disregard.
